Mancala bead

Location: trench 2, context 1

This is a small, round, clear glass bead. It appears to be a bead designed for playing mancala, a classic game that is played with small beads, stones, or other items used for counting, and holes in either a wood board or the ground.

Mancala has existed since before 6000 BCE, and is one of the oldest known games still commonly played today.[8] Modern mancala sets include a wooden board along with a set of glass beads similar to that found on our site. However, playing mancala is not the only use for mancala beads, as they can also be used for other types of counting-based games and even for decoration.

Thus, although we may be tempted to conclude that a resident of 82 Brown St was a mancala player, it is very possible that this bead has never been used for mancala, and may even not be associated with the boarding house at all. However, we can hypothesize that someone associated with either 82 Brown St or the List Art Building had a need for a simple way to keep track of a number of objects, and may potentially have used beads to count.
