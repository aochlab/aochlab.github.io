Pen

Location: trench 4, context 1

These two plastic fragments make up two halves of a ballpoint pen. The pen reads “Think Safety Boston Edison” on the side. The lower half is white, while the upper half is black, and the pen tapers to a point at the writing end.

Boston Edison, founded in 1886, was an electric company that serviced Boston and the surrounding area from the late 19th to the late 20th centuries. When it was founded, the company was officially named “Edison Electric Illuminating Company of Boston” and did not change its name to “Boston Edison” until 1937, though it was known informally as Boston Edison prior to that.[1] This pen is most likely a promotional product for Boston Edison, created to advertise the company. Plastic pens were invented in 1924, and ballpoint pens in 1931, indicating that this pen was likely produced sometime after the 1930s.[9]

It is possible that the pen was owned by a Boston Edison employee, or it may have been given out to either a Boston Edison customer or a member of the general public as part of a promotional campaign, or it could have been acquired by some other means entirely. Although we cannot determine whether this pen is associated with 82 Brown St, we know that whoever owned the pen visited either 82 Brown St or the List Art Building at some point and likely dropped the pen somewhere on our site.
