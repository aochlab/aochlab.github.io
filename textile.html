Textile

Location: trench 2, context 1

These textiles were found in context one of trench two. The textile on the left consists of two pieces of olive green ribbon with frayed ends, and the pieces have been tied together at one end. The textile on the right is a pink knit fabric made of some type of yarn. The exact materials of each textile could not be determined, but the textile on the left appears to be a fabric similar to silk or satin. The textile on the right could be made of almost anything — yarn can be made of acrylic, wool, cotton, or many other types of fibers.[10]

Both artifacts appear to be scraps of what were once larger objects, but it’s unclear exactly what those objects were. They could be garments, homemade crafts, decoration, or any variety of other textile-based objects. Given the remarkably good condition of these finds, it is unlikely that they are associated with 58 College St, and most likely were deposited on the site relatively recently.
