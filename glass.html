Glass

Location: trench 1, context 3

Along with brick and slate, glass was one of the most commonly excavated materials from the LAB trenches. This collection is from trench oneâ€™s third context, and represents only a small sampling of all the glass that was collected. Most of the glass is relatively small, flat shards of clear glass. However, there are several exceptions.

Some shards of glass appear curved, indicating that they come from a round vessel, such as a jar or cup. Other shards are fluted or ribbed, which is a common technique used to diffuse light and prevent one from seeing directly through the glass. This glass is popular with architects and interior designers to provide both light and privacy, but it is also used decoratively on a variety of glass objects. Additionally, some glass is colored rather than clear. There are several brown shards, which may come from beer bottles, and this is particularly likely considering the fact that our dig site is located on a college campus. However, the blue shards are less easily attributed to a particular object. Some bottles and jars are blue, as are a variety of glass decorations, and we cannot be sure what the source of the blue glass is. Overall, the abundance of glass indicates how common glass is in both construction and domestic goods, and makes it extremely difficult to determine a particular source for any shard of glass.
