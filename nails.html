Nails

Location: trench 2, context 2

These are three nails found in context two of trench two. Two of the nails are short and straight, while the third is longer and bent at a ninety degree angle. All of the nails are highly rusted, which is to be expected after being exposed to the elements for a significant period of time. All three nails were likely involved in some form of construction, whether it be that of 58 College St or that of the List Art Building.

The nail with the bend is particularly interesting. The nail may have been bent during use, either intentionally or unintentionally. Clinching is a woodworking technique that involves bending a nail over on itself to increase the strength of the joint between two pieces of wood, and often results in a nail with a bend in it like this.[4] However, it is also possible that the nail was bent unintentionally and discarded, as it could no longer be used, or that it was used while it was straight and bent after being deposited in the ground. We cannot be sure which is the case, but this nail definitely raises interesting questions about construction that occurred near our site.
