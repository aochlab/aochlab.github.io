Slag

Location: trench 3, context 2

This is a collection of slag found in context two of trench three. Slag is waste material that is produced as a byproduct of smelting, welding, or burning metal. It consists of a combination of stony deposits, metal oxides, and other impurities that were not fully combusted during the metal heating process.

This slag appears to be domestically produced rather than industrially, indicating that it may have come from some metal object or material being burned in a home fireplace. According to a 1940 Residential Assessment Record of 58 College St, the building had three chimneys and wood burning fireplaces. We cannot be sure exactly what process produced this slag, but we can hypothesize that it may have been produced in the fireplace of 58 College St, and deposited in the ground when the building was torn down.
